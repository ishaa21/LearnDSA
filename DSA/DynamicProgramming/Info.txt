Dynamic Programming (DP) is an algorithmic technique used to solve complex problems by breaking them into smaller overlapping subproblems and storing their results to avoid repeated calculations.

The key idea behind dynamic programming is optimization. Instead of solving the same subproblem again and again, DP stores previously solved results and reuses them when needed.

Dynamic programming works best when a problem has overlapping subproblems and optimal substructure, meaning the solution of a problem depends on solutions of smaller parts.

By saving intermediate results, DP significantly reduces time complexity compared to naive recursive approaches.
