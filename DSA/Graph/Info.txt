A graph is a data structure used to represent connections between objects. It consists of nodes (vertices) and edges that connect these nodes.

Graphs are used to model relationships where elements are linked in non-linear ways. These connections can be directed or undirected, and may also have weights representing cost, distance, or time.

Graphs are powerful because they can represent real-life networks such as social media, transportation systems, and communication networks.

Graph algorithms help in analyzing paths, connectivity, and relationships within complex systems.
