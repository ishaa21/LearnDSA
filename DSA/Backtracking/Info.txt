Backtracking is an algorithmic technique used to find solutions by trying all possible choices step by step. If a choice leads to a wrong or invalid solution, the algorithm goes back (backtracks) and tries another option.

It follows the idea of trial and error, but in a systematic way. At every step, a decision is made, and if that decision does not satisfy the required conditions, it is immediately rejected instead of continuing further.

Backtracking works by exploring a solution space in a depth-first manner. It builds a solution incrementally and abandons a path as soon as it is clear that the path cannot lead to a valid solution.

This technique is mainly used for optimization and decision problems where multiple possibilities exist and all valid solutions need to be explored efficiently.

Backtracking reduces unnecessary work by pruning invalid paths early, which makes it more efficient than brute force in many cases.
